# ORB Trading Principles

Read this every day before trading.

1. **Wait for the 10:00-10:30 window.** First-candle breakouts (09:35) have the highest KO rate. Let the dust settle. The best signals come 25-55 minutes after open.

2. **First signal only.** If you get stopped on a full reversal, do not re-enter. The move is done. Walk away from that ticker for the day.

3. **Longs over shorts.** Long signals have significantly better ROI and runner potential. Be extra selective with short entries — demand higher quality.

4. **Quality >= 2.** Don't take a signal unless at least two factors align (trend, Monday range, breakout size, volume). A lone breakout against the trend is noise.

5. **Check the gradient before holding overnight.** The SMA(20) gradient > 0.3% is your green light. No strong slope = exit at close. Strong slope in your direction = consider holding.

6. **If holding overnight, consider certificates.** Turbos with overnight KO risk eat your edge. Switch to a bull/bear certificate for the overnight portion if available.

7. **Sector ETFs are safer. Single stocks are spicier.** ETFs have lower KO rates. For individual names, favor SLV, GE, HD, INTC, WMT — they've shown the best signal quality.

8. **Accept small losses, protect big wins.** Your edge comes from the runners — the few trades held 3-5 days that move 10-20%. Most daily trades are near break-even. That's fine. Don't overtrade trying to force winners.
